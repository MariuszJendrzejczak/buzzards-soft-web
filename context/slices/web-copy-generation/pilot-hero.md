**Drift diagnosis:** The first draft (mirroring the "before") reached reflexively for
negation-first framing — the amateur hero leads on *bez ceny agencji* and *to nie
generator*, holding the bad options in the reader's mind. The rewrite's through-line
fix is a single move applied consistently: **replace every "not-X" with the "Y" it
implies** — *bez ceny agencji* → *w cenie kilkuset złotych*, *to nie generator, jest
człowiek* → *nad każdym krokiem czuwa człowiek, który odpowiada za jakość*. That one
inversion resolved dimensions 4, 6, and 9 together, because the negation, the
defensive AI-caveat, and the weak headline were all the same drift. Colloquialisms
(*pogadasz*, *zaistnieć w sieci*) and the H1 em-dash were removed in the same pass
(dims 3, 7). Ownership is deliberately stated here (hero promise) and must **not** be
re-pitched in later sections (Rule of One, dim 8).

## Open Questions

1. **Price anchor wording** — the H1 uses *"w cenie kilkuset złotych"* as the concrete
   price hook. The shipped pricing is 999 / 1999 zł (launch prices). *Kilkuset* is
   honest for Basic (999) but reads low against Full (1999). Confirm the hero should
   anchor on the entry price, or switch to a range (*"od kilkuset złotych"*) — a
   pricing/positioning decision, not a copy invention.
2. **Secondary CTA target** — *"Zobacz, jak pracuję"* points at the process/`/#warsztat`
   proof. Confirm the link target (the offer page's own `guide`/`plan` section vs. the
   parent site's `/#warsztat` anchor) so the CTA label matches the destination.
3. **Eyebrow scope** — kept audience-only (*małe firmy i freelancerzy*). Confirm whether
   the earlier *"osoby, które chcą być widoczne w sieci"* audience (individuals, not
   just firms) should stay in scope for the hero or move to the `problem` section.
