## A. What the research CONFIRMS we already do right

| Our decision | Market confirmation |
|---|---|
| **Ownership stated, no migration/"you can leave" angle** | The PL market *does* use lock-in framing (czujowski, TS-PROJEKT: "hosting w prezencie to pułapka"). Best practice is to **affirm ownership and stop** — it's "the market's biggest open lane" of trust. Our migration ban is aligned with framing theory. |
| **RODO + security on the page** | Most competitors are **silent** on RODO/security (open lane). We have `includes.security`, `includes.gdpr`, `faq.security`, `faq.gdpr` — ahead of market. |
| **Recurring domain cost labelled "rocznie"** | Rare and trust-building. The single word **"rocznie"** kills the "do I pay monthly?" fear — present in "Własność i koszty". |
| **Removed "bez zobowiązań" from near the form** | Hard data: Michael Aagaard A/B test — the word "spam" in a reassurance line **dropped conversions 18.7%**. Negative words near a form hurt. Our no-negation rule now has external evidence. |
| **6-step process** | It's the **market norm** (dominant cluster 5–7; 6 is the most common count on real offer sections). Not too many. |
| **CTA as invitation, no quote/pressure** | Best practice for trust-led buyers: one soft CTA, zero urgency. "Napisz do mnie, czego potrzebujesz" + arranging a call lands exactly right. |

## B. Section-by-section challenge (current copy vs market + Voice-Gap-Test)

**1. HERO — `heading` "Profesjonalna strona zaprojektowana dla Twojej firmy"**
- ⚠️ **VGT dim 9 (4 U's): passes Useful + Ultra-specific, fails "Unique"** — a competitor could sign their name under this headline. Market advice: name the **client's win** (getting found, looking credible, getting inquiries), not the service.
- The "profesjonalna" echo (heading ↔ subheading) still lives — known, deferred.
- **Option (not a mandate):** a more outcome-first headline variant. Owner's call (they picked this one deliberately).

**2. PROBLEM — "Dla kogo skierowana jest ta oferta"**
- ✅ Aligned with the no-contrast rule (villain beat is optional). Market confirms: **name the feeling, not a foe.**
- Optional light "internal feeling" touch ("nie wiesz, od czego zacząć — to normalne"), framed affirmatively. Minor.

**3. GUIDE (process, 6 steps) — "Jak pracuję nad Twoją stroną"**
- ⚠️ **Tension to resolve:** charter v3 says "2–3 substantive sentences per card", but process research says "**1–3-word noun heading + one line per step**" for scannability. Real difference: **process steps can be tighter than includes cards.** Worth splitting the two rules.
- 💡 **Live mockup = our genuine edge**, but say it in **plain PL**: "widzisz prawdziwą stronę w przeglądarce, na telefonie — nie obrazek strony". Check it doesn't read too technical.
- 💡 **Sell the parallelism as a timeline win** (content + build together = faster) — and that's where AI "shortens the road, doesn't change the destination" ([Human Agency](https://www.humanagency.com/ai/ai-assisted-web-desig)).

**4. PRICING — "Cennik"**
- ✅ Have: 3 tiers, Full visually distinct, leading "W cenie każdej strony" block, reduced diffs (subpages + dopracowanie), real promo numbers.
- 💡 **Missing a "Najczęściej wybierany" badge on Full** — a badge lifts that tier's conversion ~25–35% ([orbix](https://www.orbix.studio/blogs/saas-pricing-page-psychology-convert)).
- 💡 **Custom ("Ustalany indywidualnie")** — market: give it a **floor ("od X zł")** + **triggers** (sklep, integracje, CRM) so it's a path, not a dead end, and anchors Full upward ([knapsackcreative](https://knapsackcreative.com/blog/web-design/web-design-pricing)). We already name the triggers in "Granice oferty" — mirror them in pricing.
- ⚠️ **Verify promo end-date visibility** ("do końca sierpnia"). Research + our facts: the limit must be **real, dated, and visible, with a reason** ([personizely](https://www.personizely.net/blog/limited-time-offers)). If the date isn't rendered, add it.

**5. OWNERSHIP ("Własność i koszty") + FAQ**
- ✅ Strong. "Answer the question the client is afraid to ask" — the three most-**dodged** topics (ownership, real recurring cost, RODO/security) we address head-on.
- 💡 **Anchor the timeline to a client action:** market advice is "liczymy od dnia, gdy mamy komplet treści" instead of just "zależy". Our `duration` covers package + backlog + estimate-at-meeting — could add the "from complete materials" anchor.
- ➕ **Possible gap:** "after launch / support". Our policy: support = during the project only; post-launch is arranged separately. Market often has this FAQ. Optional short affirmative line — but we deliberately **do not** promise ongoing care. Owner's decision.

**6. QUOTE (CTA)** — ✅ aligned with best practice (invitation, no urgency, no quote). No change.

## C. One strategic tension (owner decision)

**"AI = cheaper" as a hook.** International research is unanimous: do **not** pass AI
savings to the client as a discount — it pushes you into commodity / "race to the bottom";
sell **judgment + accountability**, and speed as a **client benefit**
([bizwhat](https://www.bizwhat.net/p/charge-more-work-less-how-ai-lets),
[Superframeworks](https://superframeworks.com/articles/ai-slop-backlash-judgment-moat),
[selfemployed](https://www.selfemployed.com/news/ai-freelance-value-based-pricing-2026/)).

**BUT** — our positioning is a deliberate **budget segment** (micro-businesses that couldn't
afford an agency before AI). For this client, "affordable/accessible" is on-brand. Our
`problem.body3` already frames it as **access** ("firms that before the AI era couldn't
afford it"), not "cheap because machine".

**Recommendation:** keep affordability as an **access story**, but (a) AI is never the
biggest word on the page, (b) every AI mention is paired with a human + accountability (we
do this), (c) speed is framed as a client benefit. This needs a conscious owner sign-off,
not a silent change.

⚠️ **Also (design, not copy):** our own site must **not** look "AI-built" (indigo→violet
gradient, three equal cards, generic hero) — a buyer's ~50 ms verdict lands before the copy
is read, and would contradict it ([utsubo](https://www.utsubo.com/blog/built-with-ai-trust-signals-2026)).
Flag for the design-review phase.

## D. Proposed new rules for the skill + agent (PROPOSED — not yet wired)

1. **AI-transparency v2** (→ `voice-charter.md` §4): speed = client benefit, not a discount;
   AI is never the biggest word; "AI shortens the road, doesn't change the destination"; for
   the budget segment, affordability = an **access** story, always paired with human
   accountability.
2. **Anti-AI-tell hygiene** (→ `ban-list-pl.md`): the slop-superlative family
   (`seamless / robust / cutting-edge / kompleksowo / najwyższa jakość`), hedges ("może
   pomóc"), and "a hero any competitor could sign" as a hard VGT-9 fail.
3. **Concreteness-as-trust** (→ charter / VGT): numbers and lists beat adjectives; a specific
   number **is** the trust signal ("od X zł", "rocznie", a materials checklist).
4. **Objection meta-move** (→ `framework-spine.md`, FAQ): "answer the question the client is
   afraid to ask" — ownership, real recurring cost, RODO/security are the most-dodged =
   biggest trust wins.
5. **Pricing-section canon** (→ `framework-spine.md`): 3 tiers, middle/Full as the visual hero
   + "Najczęściej wybierany" badge; lead with the shared included block; 1–2 escalating
   differentiators; real numbers (no blank "od"); custom = path + floor + triggers; promo =
   real dated limit + reason; separate one-time vs recurring.
6. **Process-section canon** (→ `framework-spine.md`): scannable steps (1–3-word heading + one
   line — **looser than the 2–3-sentence rule for cards**); mockup as a decision gate; "live
   in the browser" in plain PL; parallelism = timeline win.
7. **CTA canon + affirmation evidence** (→ charter §2 / spine): one soft invitation-CTA, zero
   urgency for trust-led buyers; cite the **Aagaard −18.7%** datum as external proof of the
   no-negation-near-forms rule.
